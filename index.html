About Me

I was originally from Tijuana, Mexico, before moving to Oklahoma at a young age. There, I embarked on my educational journey at East Central University, where I earned degrees in Physics and Mathematics, with minors in Computer Science. My university experience ignited a fervent passion for research and the relentless pursuit of knowledge.

Currently, I am a second-year ACSM PhD student at the University of Notre Dame, working under the mentorship of Dr. Lindsay. We focus on numerical simulations of cellular diffusion, investigating diffusion patterns in both circular and square domains, with a particular emphasis on predefined paths. My previous projects have spanned a variety of fields, including Deep Learning, Reinforcement Learning, Operations Research, and Experimental Physics. In terms of technical skills, I am proficient in several programming languages, including Python, R, C++, and Matlab.

In addition to my academic and professional endeavors, I enjoy the great outdoors and prioritize maintaining a balanced and healthy lifestyle. Regular workouts are essential to both my physical and mental well-being. Beyond fitness, I channel my creativity and technical skills into coding projects driven by my personal passions and interests. These projects not only provide outlets for technical exploration but also serve as means to solve real-world problems, innovate, and contribute to the communities I care about.

Current/Previous Research Endeavors

My current research focuses on the fascinating intersection of mathematics and biology, exploring how mathematical principles can unravel complexities within biological systems. This interdisciplinary approach seeks to bridge the gap between abstract mathematical theories and tangible biological phenomena, aiming to uncover new insights that could revolutionize our understanding of life and its underlying mechanisms.

In the past, my interests have spanned a diverse array of fields, including but not limited to:

  • Partial Differential Equations: Linear, nonlinear PDEs and homogenization theory
  • Deep Learning: Investigating the capabilities and applications of deep neural networks in modeling complex patterns and making predictions from large datasets.
  • Reinforcement Learning: Exploring how agents can learn from interactions with their environment to achieve specific goals.
  • Experimental Physics: Engaged in hands-on laboratory experiments to probe the fundamental principles of physics, complemented by rigorous mathematical modeling to interpret experimental data and predict physical phenomena.
  • Operational Research: Applying mathematical models and analytical methods to make better decisions and solve problems in business, engineering, and other domains.

My interdisciplinary research thrives on a robust foundation in diverse coding languages, including Python, R, MATLAB, C++, and HTML. This proficiency enables me to conduct sophisticated simulations, in-depth analyses, and create dynamic visualizations of complex systems. These technical skills are essential, allowing for a seamless integration of theoretical exploration with practical application, and enhancing my ability to navigate and contribute to a broad spectrum of scientific inquiries.
